# SHORT VIDEO SCRIPT — YoutubeFocus

**Format:** 30-second screen recording with voiceover
**Platform:** TikTok, Reels, YouTube Shorts (yes, posting a focus app on Shorts — maximum irony, maximum algorithm reach)

---

## Hook (0–5s)

**Screen:** YouTube homepage — Shorts shelf visible at top, home feed packed with thumbnails, sidebar cluttered with recommendations.
**Voiceover:** "This is YouTube before YoutubeFocus. Now watch."

---

## Show (5–20s)

**Screen (5–8s):** Click the YoutubeFocus extension icon in the Chrome toolbar. The popup opens — clean dark UI, Focus Mode toggle centered, Timed Lock buttons visible below.
**Voiceover:** "One click—"

**Screen (8–11s):** Toggle Focus Mode ON. The YouTube page refreshes/updates: Shorts shelf disappears, home feed is replaced with a clean empty state, sidebar noise is gone.
**Voiceover:** "— Shorts gone. Home feed gone. All of it."

**Screen (11–15s):** Navigate to a YouTube video. Show the video player — no comments section below, no related videos panel on the right, no notification bell buzzing.
**Voiceover:** "Open a video. Nothing to click away to."

**Screen (15–20s):** Click back to the extension popup. Click "25 min" under Timed Lock. Session starts. The button now shows a countdown timer.
**Voiceover:** "Or lock it — 25, 50, 90 minutes. Distractions stay off until the session ends."

---

## Close (20–30s)

**Screen:** Split view — left side shows YouTube before (chaos), right side shows after (clean video player). Or just hold on the clean video player state for 3 seconds.
**Voiceover:** "No account. No tracking. No data leaving your browser. Just YouTube, the way it should work."
**Text overlay:** `YoutubeFocus — Free Chrome Extension`
**Voiceover:** "Free. Open source. Install link in bio."
