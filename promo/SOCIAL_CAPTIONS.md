## LinkedIn
*(2-3 short paragraphs, professional but not dry)*

I built a Chrome extension called YoutubeFocus because I kept opening YouTube to watch one video and leaving 90 minutes later.

It hides Shorts, the home feed, related video recommendations, comments, Trending, and Explore — all the features designed to keep you scrolling. One-click Focus Mode toggles them off instantly. Timed Lock lets you set 25, 50, 90, or custom-minute sessions where distractions stay hidden until you're done. No account, no remote code, no analytics — everything runs locally on your machine.

It's Chrome Web Store ready and open source. If your team uses YouTube for training videos, demos, or tutorials, this is the extension to have installed. Link in comments.
